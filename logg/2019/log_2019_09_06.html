Strategy for ice drift computation¶

  • Create a grid box bounded by parallels/meridians enclosing the A-TWAIN location (E.g. 10x10 km?). Define grids representing both the boundaries and the internal grid points. Save the grid into to a netCDF file.

  • Interpolate u, v from both NSIDC (remember to rotate) and Ifremer datasets onto:

  • Each of the boundaries.

  • Each of the grid points.

  • The exact A-TWAIN point.

  • Save results in (common netCDF file) or, actually, maybe as a dictionary.

  • Do some checks, including plotting the results geographically.

  • Compute area fluxes into the area bu integrating velocity normal to the boundaries (\(\pm\sum_i u_i dy\) (zonal), \(\pm\sum_i v_i dx\) (meridional)).

  • Compute area average drift by averaging u, v at the internal points.

  • Save time series of u_loc, u_box and fluxes (each boundary and net) to a Bunch.
