Bibliographies¶

Spent quite some time looking at this without really understanding what’s going on. The documentation for this stuff is all over the place. This seems to work for Zotero-exported bibliographies, using natbib to give a reasonable format:

\documentclass{article}

\usepackage{natbib}
\bibliographystyle{unsrtnat}

\begin{document}

..it has been said that "wings are to a bird like arms are to a
man, except men can't fly and birds can't use forks"
(\cite{young_propagation_1997})...


\bibliography{-name of .bib file-}
\end{document}

This looks like a good natbib reference sheet. I haven’t looked at it in detail.

Nice links for natbib citation and bibliography styles.

For exporting Zotero bibtex bibliography I use the Better Bibtex Zotero extension, which lets you control the export format including the citekey.
