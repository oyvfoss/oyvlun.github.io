Quick look and sanity check¶

Code

atwain/code/sea_ice_budget/first_look/first_look_sea_ice_budget.py

First look at the drift and sea ice area interpolated onto the med grid.

Trying to answer the following questions:

  1. Do the individual time series look reasonable?

  2. Do the net flux and area cover match?
    • Qualitatively?

      • E.g.: Periods of high flux in should correspond to increased SIC..

    • Quantitatively?

      • The time derivative of SIC should be a pretty good match to the net flux. In principle, any other change should be due to melting/formation..

1. Do the individual time series look reasonable?¶

Figures show daily averages and 31-day running means.

Area cover

  • A-TWAIN point SIC is slightly lower and more variable than the domain average.

  • Sea-ice covered area is proportional to average SIC (note: area weighted average).

\(\rightarrow\) Seems reasonable.

Area fluxes

  • Net flux into domain is usually positive.

  • Meridional and zonal fluxes both usually balance pretty well, and the net flux is usually much smaller than the fluxes through the individual boundaries.

\(\rightarrow\) Seems reasonable.

2. Do the net flux and area cover match?¶

  • Magnitudes are at least comparable (both datasets are pretty noisy). Generally, the flux amplitude is higher than d(SIC)/dt.

  • There is at least some correspondence between the time series - and they agree in ice-free periods.

  • However, they flux in and area change are far from balancing each other. This could be due to:

    1. Methodological errors. E.g., something wrong in the computations, or perhaps it is not appropriate to directly compare these products?

    2. Actual, physical processes (melting/freezing).

Not sure exatcly what to expect here, a priori, or how to evaluate whether this methodology really makes sense..
